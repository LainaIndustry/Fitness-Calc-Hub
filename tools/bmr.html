About BMR

Your Basal Metabolic Rate represents the number of calories your body would burn if you stayed in bed all day. It's the energy needed for basic bodily functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production.

Factors that affect BMR:

  • Body size and composition: Larger bodies and more muscle mass increase BMR
  • Age: BMR typically decreases with age
  • Gender: Men usually have higher BMR than women
  • Genetics: Some people are born with faster metabolisms
  • Hormones: Thyroid function and other hormones affect BMR

Note: This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, which is considered the most accurate BMR formula for most people.
