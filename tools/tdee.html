About TDEE

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is an estimate of how many calories you burn per day when exercise is taken into account. It's calculated by first figuring out your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), then multiplying that value by an activity multiplier.

TDEE Components:

  • Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR): Calories burned at complete rest
  • Thermic Effect of Food (TEF): Calories burned digesting food
  • Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (EAT): Calories burned during exercise
  • Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT): Calories burned during daily activities

Note: This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation for BMR, which is considered the most accurate for most people.
