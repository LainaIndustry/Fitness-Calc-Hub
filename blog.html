Understanding BMI: What Your Number Really Means

Published on December 15, 2023 • 5 min read

Body Mass Index (BMI) is one of the most commonly used tools to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight. But what does your BMI number actually tell you, and what are its limitations?

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in meters squared. While it's a useful screening tool, it doesn't account for factors like muscle mass, bone density, and distribution of fat.

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 to 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 to 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or greater

Remember that BMI is just one indicator of health. Athletes with high muscle mass may have a high BMI but low body fat, while older adults may have a normal BMI but high body fat percentage.

How to Use Your BMR and TDEE for Weight Management

Published on December 10, 2023 • 7 min read

Understanding your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is crucial for effective weight management. These numbers form the foundation of any successful nutrition plan.

Your BMR represents the calories your body needs at complete rest, while your TDEE accounts for your activity level. The difference between these numbers and your calorie intake determines whether you lose, maintain, or gain weight.

Practical Applications:

  • Weight loss: Consume 300-500 calories less than your TDEE
  • Weight maintenance: Match your calorie intake to your TDEE
  • Weight gain: Consume 300-500 calories more than your TDEE

The Importance of Hydration: Beyond the 8-Glass Rule

Published on December 5, 2023 • 4 min read

We've all heard the advice to drink eight glasses of water per day, but hydration needs are highly individual. Factors like body weight, activity level, climate, and overall health all influence how much water you need.

Proper hydration affects nearly every system in your body, from cognitive function to joint health and temperature regulation. Even mild dehydration can impact your energy levels and physical performance.

Signs of Adequate Hydration:

  • Light yellow urine
  • Regular urination
  • Moist mouth and skin
  • Consistent energy levels
